From Exclusion to Accountability

A history of anti-racism, equity, inclusion and accessibility in the Public Service of Canada. Every entry names what moved things forward and what fell short, with a source you can check.

The pattern in one sentence: the reforms that produced durable change were written into law, were enforceable, measured results with disaggregated data, and had organized employees keeping pressure on. Announcements without those four traits tended to fade.

This is an independent educational resource, not a Government of Canada site. Links were last checked in July 2026. Reports that predate the web are cited by title and year; most can be found through publications.gc.ca or the Library of Parliament. If you spot an error, verify against the linked primary source and report it to the site maintainer.
