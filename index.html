From Exclusion to Accountability

A history of anti-racism, equity, inclusion and accessibility in the Public Service of Canada. Every entry names what changed and what fell short, with a link to the source.

Display

For more than a century, people inside Canada's federal public service have pushed the institution to hire fairly, promote fairly, and treat every employee with dignity. This site traces that record: the laws, commissions, task forces, audits, court and tribunal cases, and employee networks that tried to remove barriers for Indigenous, Black and racialized employees, women, persons with disabilities, and 2SLGBTQIA+ public servants.

The pattern that emerges is consistent. Barriers get named, commitments get made, and follow-through decides everything. The tools that produced durable change share four traits: they were written into law, they were enforceable, they measured results with disaggregated data, and organized employees kept pressure on. Announcements without those traits tended to fade.

This is an independent educational resource. It is not a Government of Canada site. Links were checked in July 2026. Reports that predate the web are cited by title and year; most can be found through publications.gc.ca or the Library of Parliament. If you spot an error, please verify against the linked primary source.
